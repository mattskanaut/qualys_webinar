# SASIG Webinar Series - Titles and Blurbs

## Series title

The New Normal: Software, Security and the AI Stack
 
AI is already in your stack, writing your code, running in your products, and embedded in the vendor tools your teams depend on. This two-part series offers security leaders a practical view of what has changed, and what to do next.
 
Part 1 - The New Software Development Lifecycle: Security When Every Developer Writes With AI
 
We are in the midst of the biggest shift in software development since the invention of the compiler. Developers at Anthropic and OpenAI have not written a line of code by hand for months. Your organisation will use AI development tools, or be left far behind in the feature race. Is your secure-by-design development process ready for this shift? Let's explore the implications for secure software development, where the new risks are piling up, and what CISOs and engineering leaders should be doing about it.
 
Part 2 - Running AI in Production: What You Are Actually Defending Now
 
Your business just shipped an AI feature. What exactly are you defending, and how? Prompt injection, model supply-chain attacks, agent identity sprawl, and AI tooling buried inside vendor platforms are all rewriting what "production security" means. Most enterprises are inventing the discipline as they go. We will work through what to inventory, what to test, what to govern, and the questions you should be putting to your teams before the next launch.
